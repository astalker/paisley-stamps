Welcome to the Paisley Philatelic Society

Regrettably, due to dwindling membership and attendances at meetings plus the after effects of the COVID-19 pandemic, it has been decided that Paisley Philatelic Society can no longer continue to function. Hence, as of end of August 2021, Paisley Philatelic Society has ceased operations.

It was agreed by the remaining members that we could not simply just struggle on so it was decided that we should just give up the fight. However, as with all other clubs, not just in philately, it has been a struggle to maintain and attract new members and, despite moving to afternoon meetings, attendances have dropped off noticeably. Sadly we cannot keep going. A number of the members are members of other societies so they are not lost to the hobby. Sadly we lost two members of the Society in the last two years due to ill-health (not COVID-19). Paisley Philatelic Society (formed in 1947) existed to encourage interest in and the growth of philately and the fellowship which arose from it. Philately (derived from the Greek philos meaning loving and ateles meaning franked) is the collection of and study of all aspects of postage stamps and postal history. There were no age restrictions nor minimum collection standards for membership of the Society.

Members met fortnightly in the Ralston Community Centre at 2pm on Wednesday afternoons. A typical meeting would involve display of philately material by a guest or guests from another society, or by one or more of the Society's own members. The display would be either a collection of stamps, postal history covers or postcards, or a theme illustrated and developed using what is printed on stamps along with other appropriate ephemera.
